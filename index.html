Popular destinations In Jordan


The city of Petra, the capital of the Nabataean Arabs, is considered one of the most famous archaeological sites in the world and the most important tourist attraction in Jordan, as it is visited by groups of tourists from all over the world. It is located 262 kilometers south of Amman. Petra is distinguished by the nature of its architecture carved into the pink rock, which contains... A mixture of ancient architectural arts belonging to various civilizations, it is an entire city carved into pink rock.

The city of Jerash sits on the throne of tourism in Jordan, as it includes a large number of archaeological monuments that are worth visiting, such as squares, temples, cathedrals, theatres, amphitheatres, baths, and many others. The city still preserves its charming Roman archaeological landmarks, which gave the city its special architectural character. Among the most important tourist attractions in it, for example, are the Street of Columns, the Northern Coliseum, the Umayyad Mosque, the Shrine of the Prophet David, the Temple of Artemis, and the Jerash Museum of Antiquities. One of the most important activities that you can do in Jerash is to visit the horse arena and watch training, chariot racing and other various shows presented in the arena.

Aqaba Castle is an ancient castle located in the center of the city of Aqaba in southern Jordan, specifically on the shore of the Gulf of Aqaba. This castle dates back to the end of the Mamluk era in the region, but today it has been transformed into a museum. One of the most prominent landmarks in the citadel is the Aqaba Mast. It was built in 2004 in the square adjacent to the citadel to carry the flag of the Great Arab Revolt. At that time, it was the tallest mast in the world.
